I am a Ph.D. candidate in economics at the University of Michigan and an Institute of Education Sciences predoctoral fellow. My research focuses on labor economics, the economics of education, and public policy.

Using large administrative data from the education, child welfare, and criminal justice systems, I seek to understand how public institutions influence the lives of historically underserved children.

I am on the job market in 2019-2020 and will be available for interviews at the 2019 APPAM Fall Research Conference in Denver and the 2020 ASSA Meeting in San Diego.
